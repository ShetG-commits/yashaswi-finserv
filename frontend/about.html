Our Story

Yashaswi Finserv is an AMFI-registered Mutual Fund Distributor (MFD) operating under the leadership of Pavan Tavargeri. We are authorized to distribute mutual fund products and facilitate access to various mutual fund schemes for investors.

Our Role

As an AMFI-registered Mutual Fund Distributor, our services are limited to the distribution and facilitation of mutual fund products. We provide information about mutual fund schemes and assist investors in accessing these products. We do not provide personalized investment advice unless separately registered as a SEBI-registered Investment Advisor (RIA).

Our Services

We facilitate access to various mutual fund products including equity funds, debt funds, hybrid funds, and tax-saving schemes. Our role includes providing product information, assisting with the distribution process, and supporting investors in understanding available mutual fund options.

Important Note

Yashaswi Finserv operates as a Mutual Fund Distributor and not as a SEBI-registered Investment Advisor. We do not provide personalized investment advice. Any information shared is for educational and informational purposes only. Investors are advised to make their own investment decisions based on their financial situation, risk tolerance, and investment objectives. Investors should read all scheme-related documents carefully before investing.

Risk Factors: Mutual funds are subject to market risk because their value can rise or fall unpredictably due to various factors such as economic changes, interest rate fluctuations, or global events like political unrest or natural disasters. Market risk affects all investments, as it is linked to the overall market and economy. Types of market risk include equity risk, interest rate risk, credit risk, inflation risk, sociopolitical risk, and country risk. The specific market risks impacting a mutual fund depend on the assets it holds. Even well-performing companies can suffer losses during broad market declines. Therefore, while mutual funds offer diversification, they are not risk-free and should be approached with caution.
