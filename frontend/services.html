Important Risk Statement

Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks. Past performance is not indicative of future returns. Mutual fund schemes do not assure or guarantee any returns. There is no guarantee that the investment objective of any scheme will be achieved. All investors are advised to read all scheme-related documents carefully, including the Scheme Information Document (SID), Statement of Additional Information (SAI), and Key Information Memorandum (KIM), before making any investment decision. Investors should also evaluate exit loads and other cost structures (Total Expense Ratio - TER) applicable at the time of investment.

Risk Factors: Mutual funds are subject to market risk because their value can rise or fall unpredictably due to various factors such as economic changes, interest rate fluctuations, or global events like political unrest or natural disasters. Market risk affects all investments, as it is linked to the overall market and economy. Types of market risk include equity risk, interest rate risk, credit risk, inflation risk, sociopolitical risk, and country risk. The specific market risks impacting a mutual fund depend on the assets it holds. Even well-performing companies can suffer losses during broad market declines. Therefore, while mutual funds offer diversification, they are not risk-free and should be approached with caution.
